Canopy is the only chain infrastructure where "sovereign" actually means what people think it means. A chain that can leave its security provider by governance. A chain that owns its fees, its token, its rules. The tweet put it better than any whitepaper: "Your chain on Canopy can do something no other chain can: Leave."

415K plus chains launched on testnet. 254K users. $8.5M from Arrington, Fenbushi, Borderless, SNZ. The Tanssi acquisition that shipped the production plumbing. And a thesis, laid out in that June 17 tweet, that the first truly autonomous agent will want a chain of its own. Canopy is the only project building for that day.

The demo gets the attention. The architecture keeps it. These four partnerships translate that architecture into distribution, validation, and ecosystem. Zero marketing spend required.

04

EigenLayer

The shared security play. Complementary restaking models, complementary reach.
Why Canopy
Canopy's recursive restaking mesh and EigenLayer's AVS model are complementary, not competitive. An integration could let Canopy chains tap Ethereum's security pool via EigenLayer, or let AVS operators validate Canopy chains. The combined security surface dwarfs either one alone.
Why EigenLayer
Canopy brings 415K chains worth of security demand. EigenLayer brings Ethereum's $15B plus restaked security supply. A bridge between the two, Canopy chains as AVS consumers or AVS operators as Canopy validators, creates the deepest security pool in crypto.
